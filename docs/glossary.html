Human Behavior Genetics

PSYC 408, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign

Jaime Derringer's Behavior Genetics Glossary

This glossary is far from complete; it is being built up over the course of the semester.

Dizygotic (DZ, fraternal) twins
Result from the independent fertilization of two eggs by two sperm. Share on average 50% of their genes (that vary among any/all humans), just like full-sibs (but happen to be born at the same time).
Environment
Everything other than the genotype.
Genotype
Sequence of As, Cs, Ts, and Gs in DNA
Heritability (A)
Proportion (%) of phenotypic variance within a group that can be attributed to differences in genotypes. Extent to which more genetically similar individuals tend to be more phenotypically similar. Implied if, on average, identical twins are more similar to one another than fraternal twins are.
Monozygotic (MZ, identical) twins
Result from the fertilization of a single egg by a single sperm that then splits. Shared 100% of their genetic material.
Phenotype
Unnecessarily fancy word for "outcome/measure we're interested in"; can be LITERALLY anything OTHER THAN the genotype
Common or Shared environment (C)
Experiences that make individuals more similar to one another, regardless of how genetically similar they are. Implied if identical twins and fraternal twins are equally similar.
Unique or Non-shared environment (E)
Experiences that make individuals more different from one another, regardless of how genetically similar they are. Implied where identical twins are not literally exactly the same as one another. Includes measurement errors, experiences that are objectively unique, and experiences that are subjectively unique (i.e. perception of/response to the experience matters).
Variance
The extent of differences, usually (in the context of behavior genetics) between individuals
